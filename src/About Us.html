What is SLEVS?

Travel formalities for foreign Citizens Travelling to Sri Lanka. Individuals who wish
to travel to Sri Lanka are exempt from visa requirements. This is made possible under the Visa Waiver
Program (VWP) of which the Sri Lanka is a participant. When travelling to Sri Lanka all individuals will
however need to obtain an approvalby SLEVS(Sri Lankan Electronic Visa System) before they will be allowed
to enter the country.The Sri Lankan government saw it fit to set up a pre-screening system for all
travelers from Visa Waiver countries. Thus the Sri Lankan Electronic Visa System or SLEVS was born
and became a mandatory component of the Visa Waiver Program on January 12, 2009. The SLEVS program
is an online, e visa application system that allows the Department of immigration to pre-screen all
travelers who enjoy exemption from VISA requirementsbefore they board a SL bound airplane or ship.

SLEVS for Authorization Tourist Trip or Short-Term Business Trip

The application process for SLEVS can take up to a few weeks, although the SLEVS Website recommends
that the application be made at least 72 hours before departure. We recommend that you avoid applying
in rush, take some time to ensure that you understand the application process, and verify your validity
for an SLEVS before applying. If your application is denied, you will have to apply for a B-1 Visitor
Visa or B-2 Tourist Visa.

The SLEVS is a prerequisite for citizens of VWP member countries who wish to travel by air or sea to
Sri Lanka as a final destination or in transit to another destination. There's no guarantee that you
will gain permission to enter SL even when granted a travel authorization. Remember that it is not a
visa. Sri Lankan immigration will conduct checks to ascertain the validity of your travel authorization.

There are four steps to the SLEVS application process. You will first need to download the SLEVS form
then fill in the required data and submit the form electronically. The next step is to submit the required
payment. You will then receive a confirmation email reflecting the final decision. This will one of
three responses: Authorization Approved, Authorization Pending or Travel Not Authorized.

SLEVS is valid for entry into SL for two years, but you are only allowed to stay a maximum of ninety
days per visit. This 90 days is calculated from the day immigration grants you access and you enter the
country. In case of you have lost your SLEVS, you can click on the link for further informations. You can
also have a look at our SLEVS glossary for explanations.
